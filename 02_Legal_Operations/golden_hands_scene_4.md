# GoldenHands Series
## Act I: The Awakening of the Soil
### Scene 4: The Perimeter Enforcement

### Setting
The boundary line where the Puna ridge meets the lower access road. The portable sound system is live, humming with a deep, vibrating sub-bass. The youth group continues setting Kalo huli into the stone pockets behind the line.

### Audio Textures
The heavy polyrhythmic drumming abruptly stops as a county vehicle pulls up. Only the 432Hz sub-bass remains, a low, intense vibration shaking the air. 

---

### [SCENE START]

*(AGENT HARRIS steps out of the vehicle, clipboard in hand, squinting against the Puna sun. He walks up to the boundary line, visibly uncomfortable with the vibrating air pressure from the speakers. KAI and MALAMA stand right at the edge of the cultivated zone.)*

**AGENT HARRIS**
*(Shouting slightly over the sub-bass hum)*
Who's in charge of this operation? I'm with the zoning enforcement division. We received a report of unpermitted heavy landscaping and community gathering. I have an administrative stop-work order here—you can't be altering the topography of this basalt ridge without a structural engineering clearance.

**KAI**
*(Stepping forward, hands stained with cinder dust)*
Topography? We aren't building a subdivision, Harris. We are clearing invasive grass and putting Kalo back into the ground where it belongs. Look behind you—the youth are learning how to feed themselves. You're calling a living garden a zoning violation?

**AGENT HARRIS**
*(Looking down at his clipboard, avoiding eye contact with the youth)*
Look, I don't make the ordinances. The county code classifies any unauthorized structural modification of raw lava beds as non-conforming land use. If you don't cease cultivation and pack up this audio equipment, I'll have to log a formal civil violation with the state.

**MALAMA**
*(Steps directly into the sound field, his voice calm, carrying the rhythmic cadence of a Griot)*
You brought a ledger written in an office across the island to a ridge that has been memorized by our ancestors for a thousand years. Let the record show exactly what is happening here today. 

*(MALAMA signals to the upper ridge. The djembe and pahu drums drop back in instantly, locked into an intense, driving rhythm that cuts through the legal tension.)*

**MALAMA**
*(Speaking directly into the microphone, his voice echoing over the perimeter line)*
We are invoking the Griot's Protocol. This is not real estate; this is an active community sanctuary. Under the constitutional protections of traditional and customary practice, the act of planting Kalo and passing oral history to the next generation is a non-negotiable right. Log your violation, Harris. Write our names in your ledger. But tell your directors that the roots are already in the stone, and we aren't pulling them out.

*(The youth group joins the rhythm, chanting a traditional planting cadence. AGENT HARRIS steps back toward his vehicle, the corporate clipboard visibly shaking from the vibration of the sub-bass.)*

### [SCENE END]
